Religion

What religion is all about

Vasu pulled into the Hindu temple parking lot. It is Sunday morning and people are slowly arriving at the temple. As Vasu started walking towards the temple, he sees a familiar face, Mr Narasimhan, the elderly gentleman, get out of the car escorted by his wife. His wife was walking briskly beside him and Mr Narasimhan was carrying a bag in each hand. Vasu jogged over to him to help, Sir, let me help you with those.

Thank you, Vasu. How are you doing today? While they were exchanging courtesies, Mr Narasimhans wife interrupts, I am going to the sannadhi and once pooja is over, I will meet you near the front door. You arent going to attend the pooja anyway. Without waiting for his response, she walks towards the second floor to the sannadhi. Vasu silently follows Mr Narasimhan. After a couple of minutes he says, Sir, I hope you do not think I am nosey. I have noticed that you rarely participate in the pooja. I mostly see you on the first floor talking to people, but you dont seem to go upstairs. Mr Narasimhan smiles coyly and replies, We can talk about this later, come with me.

Mr Narasimhan slowly walks through the narrow corridor towards the rear of the temple where the kitchen and Bala Vihar classrooms are located. As the two near the kitchen, Amy, one of the cleaning ladies at the temple, comes out of a side room with a bucket full of cleaning products. When she sees Mr Narasimhan, she stops, and with a big smile on her face, waits for Mr Narasimhan to walk closer. Good Morning Sir. How are you today? I am great Ms Amy, how are you and how is Carlos? Is he improving in his health? Amy answers, Sir! Carlos is doing fine and is recovering well. In a couple of weeks, he should be able to return to school. Mr. Narasimhan turns to Vasu, Vasu, in the blue bag, there are couple of boxes of medicines. Would you kindly take them out and hand them over to Ms Amy?

Vasu puts his hand inside the bag, takes out several sealed boxes and hands them over to Ms Amy, who seemed to expect them. With tears swelling in her eyes, Amy says, Sir, you are so very kind. I do not know how much money you had spent on all these medicines. Since my son became sick, you have been so kind to us. I know the medicines must cost you much. I am very grateful to you. Mr Narasimhan takes Amys hand in both his hands, I am very happy that Carlos is doing well. That is all that matters. Let God be with you and your son.

As Mr Narasimhan is taking leave of Amy, Gopal, the temple cook, comes running from the kitchen towards Mr Narasimhan. Namaskaram, Sir. Gopal, there you are, Mr Narasimhan sounds as though he had been waiting for Gopal all along. Without pause he begins, Lakshmi, your daughter, is starting college in a couple of weeks, right? Gopal, with a vigorous nod of his head, responds, All because of your efforts, Sir. But for your guidance and counseling, I could never afford to send Lakshmi to a college. I am very grateful to you for helping her apply to those scholarships. Mr Narasimhan, I am not the one who worked hard and got good grades. She deserves all the credit.

Changing the subject, Mr Narasimhan says, By the way, last week when I was at the temple, I overheard Lakshmi saying something about needing a laptop because the college requires all business majors to bring a laptop to classes. I had a spare one at home. Vasu, would you kindly hand over the black computer bag to Mr Gopal, please? Mr. Gopal, with both hands held together as a mark of respect, reacts, Anna! To Lakshmi and me, you are God in a human form (Daivam Manusha Rupena). Even without our asking you help, you have helped us. I do not know how to express my gratitude. Mr. Narasimhan, shaking his head vigorously, responds, No need to thank me. I had an extra one; why not share? Just as Gopal opens his to mouth to speak, Mr Narasimhan turns to walk away.

Mr Narasimhan and Vasu continue their walk and end up back in the parking lot. Vasu, I have one more person to meet my good friend Bhairavan and then we will be on our way. Vasu is just glad to be part of the action. Wondering why Bhairavan would be waiting for him in a parking lot, Vasu follows Mr Narasimhan to his next meeting.

As they near the end of the parking lot, a scrubby little dog, his tail shaking so hard that his hind legs hardly touched the ground, comes charging from behind the garbage dumpster. Mr Narasimhan bends down, pats the dog on its forehead, and scrubs the divinely-named creature behind his ears. Bhairava, how are you doing today? Always good to see you fellow; staying out of trouble, I hope. Mr Narasimhan chuckles as if it was a private joke between Bhairavan and him. Vasu, would you mind taking out the plastic box with a yellow lid? It has a few of Bhairavans favorites. You can just leave it on the floor there.

Bhairavan starts gobbling the food. Vasu would have sworn that the dog was smiling as it occasionally lifted his head and looked at Mr Narasimhan with gratitude. After Bhairavan finishes eating, Mr Narasimhan pets the mutt again and says goodbye. The two men retrace their steps towards the temple. Vasu turns back and sees Bhairavan still standing and wagging his tail, as if to say Thank you so much, kind Sir. You are my favorite human. Mr Narasimhan is fast becoming Vasus favorite human, too.

As Vasu and Mr Narasimhan re-enter the temple, they notice Dr Mahesh, one of the cardiologists who funded the building of the temple, walking a few feet in front of them. Mahesh, Mr Narasimhan calls out. Dr Mahesh turns his head and immediately smiles. Sri Narasimhan! I was just looking for you; I wanted to say thank you. Mr Narasimhan, with a quizzical expression, responds, What could this 80-year-old man possibly have done for you? My cancer is in remission and there is no metastasis. I just have one more round of chemo to go. I should be able to get back to my practice soon. Your sincere prayers are what saved me, replies Dr Mahesh. Mr Narasimhan, with humility, counters, Mahesh, you have saved hundreds of people. God will always recognize the services of good people like you. My prayers are just to remind God of your service.

Dr Mahesh and Mr Narasimhan, together, begin walking in front of Vasu. Mr Narasimhan turns back and smiles at Vasu. Now it is time for us to visit the sannadhi.

They get to the sannadhi just as the pooja rituals are ending and the priest is ready to show the arathi. Mr Narasimhans wife is in the front of the line waiting to receive the arathi, but he stands back. Closing his eyes and after a few minutes of silent prayer, he turns to Vasu and says as a matter of fact, I am done; let us go down and wait for my wife there.

Vasu is mildly confused. Sir, you spent over an hour taking care of all those people, and even a dog. But, you spent just two minutes at the sannadhi. Vasu, it is not about how long you pray. He nods fondly towards his wife. To Janaki, bhakti and devotion are expressed through rituals. I, on the other hand, believe in Karma yoga. To me, serving the bhaktha is my way of praying. Hinduism is about compassion and caring for others and serving without expectation of reward. Until your last day on this planet, it is religious to take care of others. You are here to serve with devotion and without judgment. That is the essence of Karma yoga. Mr Narasimhan, noticing Vasus smug expression, whispers, I know youre wondering why I still visit the sannadhi then. I do to give my own personal thanks to God.
