For those of us living in Western countries, including young Indians, India is a mystery. The religion, too ritualistic, the music, too complex, and the art, anachronistic. Are they really?

India is a treasure house of myths and symbols. India has used stories and subliminal messages to convey great truths about human life and to communicate aesthetic and philosophical values that go far beyond the comprehension of ordinary individuals. When we let Indian mythological tales go unnoticed, the images and religious symbols undeciphered, and the music unfathomed, our generation would lose treasures passed on to us for four thousand years. But when we inquire and understand, we find that India, through its music, mythology, and arts, presents us with riches that defy the collective contributions of several civilizations.

This website is a humble attempt at linking Indian religion, music, and the art to the mundane life. My objective is highlight, through simple stories and illustrations, the contributions Indian religion, music, and art make to every day living. I have avoided discussions of complex musical theories or metaphysical discourses because, everyone, regardless of their training in music or the arts, should find the content useful to the conduct of their day to day life. The website is specifically directed at young people living outside India -- to encourage them, regardless of their origin or religious preferences, to explore and benefit from the values that transcend time and space.

I wish to state that the interpretations that I provide on Indian mythology, music and art are not made in any authoritative manner. They only represent my view points and my personal experiences. Learned scholars could disagree with my explanations and interpret them differently. I welcome alternative views. Regardless of what direction we take, the goal is the same -- enrich our understanding and benefit from the values.

Ram S. Sriram, Ph.D., CPA
