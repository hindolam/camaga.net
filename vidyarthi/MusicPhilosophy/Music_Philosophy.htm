Saint Thyagaraja's Philosophy through Music

Saint Thyagaraja was born on May 4th, 1767 in Thiruvarur, a village near Tanjore in Tamilnadu, India. He is perhaps the most renowned composer in Indian classic music with over 3,000 compositions to his credit. He is known as one of the Trinities of Carnatic music. Thyagaraja is also a Vaggeyakara or a poet who, not only wrote the lyrics but also set the lyrics to music. Though many of his compositions were in praise of his personal God, Sri Rama, Thyagaraja's compositions were known more for its profound philosophy and universal messages that transcend time. Saint Thyagaraja was also a revolutionary who opposed discrimination of people based on caste, creed, or gender.

 

  • Thyagaraja's biography: Thyagaraja's life exemplifies how one must stick to the path of truth and virtue for "their" own sake and not for fame, comfort, and vanity.
  • Nadopasana is worship through music. Music can lead to liberation in life. Through his compositions, Thyagaraja teaches us the nature and purpose of music.

 

  • Music and Devotion: Music and worship both should be practised with humility. Mere scholarship without humility is like decorating a dead man. It is useless.
  • The Music of Hindostan: an article by Fox Strangways on Saint Thygaraja.

 

  • On Ego and Rituals: Thyagaraja Swami tells us that it is primarily the mind that one must tame so that its full potential raises one ultimately to a Supra-personal level.
  • On Knowledge: It is not worth acquiring knowledge which does not improve the quality of man. A very well read man who has not benefited from his erudition and knowledge is like a donkey carrying a load of books.

 

  • Musical Plays: Thyagaraja also created two musical plays, commonly called operas: Prahlada Bhakthi Vijaya, and Nowka Charitha.
  • Forms of Music: The compositions included many forms - from the simplest to the complicated and allowed the novice to the learned scholar to enjoy his music and musical content.
  • On prayer: Another important ingredient of prayer is love of God and fellow human beings.

 

  • On religion and rituals: His advice to the seeker after salvation is to find the ultimate through purity of thought, humility, compassion, and love for all fellow human beings: and, all that is in one's environment.
  • The Pancharathnas: The five gems as they are called are compositions full of messages on human conduct and behavior.
  • Divya Nama Kirtanas (for group singing) The divyanama keertana themes are based on the Ramayana but as pointed out earlier, there are also songs on human conduct.
  • His Message: Sri Thyagaraja Swami emphasizes again and again, the need for high values and consistency in living up to those values.

 

 

  • Proverbs and Emotions in his compositions: Thyagaraja Swami also uses a number of proverbs in his compositions to emphasize the underlying meaning in his compositions.
  • Kshetra Compositions: refer to compositions in praise of a temple of a specific town or place. Many of Thyagaraja Swami's kshetra kirtanas are very popular.

  • The Genius: Thyagaraja's contribution to music is unique. No other composer has sung about the grammar and mystic significance of sangita in such terms as Swmi has.

 

  • The Ganaraga Pancharathnas: These five major works of art and philosophy have now, by popular usage, become an essential part of the Thyagaraja Aradhana celebrations wherever they are held.
  • Music and Worship: The emphasis on both is humility (Vinaya) and devotion, and the giving up of pride and vanity.

 

  • The Poet: Thyagaraja Swami’s compositions are described as poetry set to music. He has considerably enriched Telugu literature of the 19th century.
  • The Musicologist: Thyagaraja Swami created many new ragas. Many scholars however believe that he activated or unearthed many ragas which has been labeled and were lying dormant.
